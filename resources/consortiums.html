Huma-Num Consortiums

Thematic research communities within Huma-Num

What is a Consortium?

Within Huma-Num, consortiums are officially recognised communities of researchers and institutions that collaborate around a specific theme or discipline. They are supported by Huma-Num to:

  • Share and harmonise methodologies and standards across the community
  • Produce and curate disciplinary data repositories
  • Publish guides, thesauri, and reference documentation
  • Organise training sessions and study days (journées d’études)
  • Participate in national and European working groups
TipFor doctoral students

Depending on your discipline, one or more consortiums may be directly relevant to your research. Getting involved early connects you to a community of specialists and to resources that can help structure your data management practices from the beginning.

🔗 Full directory: https://www.huma-num.fr/les-consortiums-scientifiques/

Active Consortiums

🖼 PICTORIA — Images and Visual Sources

Best practices for digitising, describing, and sharing image collections in history, art history, and iconography. Covers metadata schemas, controlled vocabularies, and IIIF integration.

🔗 https://pictoria.huma-num.fr

📦 ARIANE — Archives

Supports archival research communities in the digital management and valorisation of archival sources. Covers archival description standards (EAD, RiC), interoperability, and open data.

🔗 https://ariane.huma-num.fr

🏛 3D-SHS — 3D for the Humanities

Facilitates the use of 3D modelling and scanning technologies for humanistic and heritage research. Provides methodological guides for acquisition, processing, and archiving of 3D data.

🔗 https://3d-shs.huma-num.fr

📐 PTM — Photogrammetry and Technical Measurements

Brings together researchers working with photogrammetry, multispectral imaging, and related techniques for the study of material culture and heritage objects.

⚗️ MASA+ — Multidisciplinary Archaeological Science Archives

Focuses on interoperability and long-term preservation of archaeological data. Strong connections to international heritage data standards (CIDOC-CRM, ARK).

🔗 https://masa.huma-num.fr

🏷 DISTAM — Stamps and Heraldic Data

Specialises in the treatment and valorisation of stamp collections, heraldic data, and related visual records, particularly for historical and diplomatic studies.

🎭 CANEVAS — Performing Arts Archives

Dedicated to the digitisation, encoding, and accessibility of performing arts archives (theatre, dance, music performance) using standards such as TEI and MEI.

🎵 MUSICA2 — Music and Musicology

Supports musicological research through digital editions, musical corpora, and specialised databases, with connections to RILM and other international bibliographic resources.

🔗 https://musica.huma-num.fr

🗣 CORLI — Spoken Language Corpora

Focuses on the collection, annotation, archiving, and dissemination of oral language corpora in French and other languages. Connected to the CLARIN infrastructure for language resources.

🔗 https://corli.huma-num.fr

🧬 SOLIFE — Biographical Data

Supports research on biographical sources and data, including prosopography. Promotes standards for data interoperability in biographical and genealogical studies.

🔗 https://solife.huma-num.fr
